Be Curious. Be Genuine. Keep It Short.
Building Your Pitch

Then ask for the meeting. It's not just what you say, it's how you show up.

1
Who you are. Your name, your company, and your connection to their community. "Hi, my name is ____. I am with Propelr and I work with businesses in this area." Merchants do business with people they recognize.
2
Why you're there. Get them talking, ask about their business, that's the whole point of stopping by. Who handles your payments? What do you like about it? Merchants open up when they feel heard.
3
Respect their time. Keep it quick, let it flow naturally. "I don't want to take up too much of your time, I just wanted to introduce myself." Brief and genuine beats long and polished.
4
Set up the meeting. After a natural conversation, ask to come back, two days, two different times. "I'd love to come back Tuesday at 9am or Thursday at 2pm, which works better?" Ask for their statements.
Delivering It

Be excited, authentic, and brief

Energy is contagious. Merchants feel it immediately.

Keep it simple

If you can't say it in 30 seconds, practice more.

Do not overtalk

Silence after your pitch is okay. Let them respond.

Remember why you're there

Schedule the meeting, not close a deal, not explain every feature.
