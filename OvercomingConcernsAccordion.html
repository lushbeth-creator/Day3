Practice Makes Confident
Overcoming Common Concerns

Practice setting the appointment with a merchant and overcoming a few common concerns below. Lean on what makes Propelr different, and remember you are showing up as their local Senior Payments Advisor, not just another rep. Tap a concern to open it.

I am happy with my current provider
How to Respond
  • Acknowledge it. "That's great, glad to hear it's working for you."
  • Clarify. Ask what they like most, and if there is anything they would change if they could.
  • Respond as their local Senior Payments Advisor, offer a no-pressure second opinion. Even a provider that is working fine can be missing something a fresh set of eyes would catch.
  • Confirm a low-stakes next step, a quick statement review rather than asking them to switch on the spot.
Practice It
Role-play turning "I'm happy" into an opening for a free statement review, without it sounding like you are calling their choice wrong.
My friend handles this for me
How to Respond
  • Acknowledge the relationship. "That's great, it's nice to have someone you trust."
  • Clarify. Ask if their friend is in payments full time, or handling it on the side alongside another job.
  • Respond by positioning yourself as a dedicated, local advisor who is reachable and focused on this one piece of their business, not squeezing it in between other things.
  • Confirm that you are not asking them to end that relationship, just offering yourself as another resource in their corner.
Practice It
Role-play respecting the friendship while still making the case for a dedicated local advisor who is easy to reach.
I work with my bank
How to Respond
  • Acknowledge the trust they have in their bank.
  • Clarify. Ask if the bank is who they call for equipment issues, pricing reviews, and day-to-day support, or if that gets referred out to someone else.
  • Respond by explaining that payments is your entire focus, not one product among many, and that you are local and available in a way a bank's call center is not.
  • Confirm by offering to be the specialist on their team alongside the bank, not a replacement for it.
Practice It
Role-play the difference between a bank as a generalist and Propelr as a dedicated, local payments specialist.
I just switched
How to Respond
  • Acknowledge the decision, no pressure to switch again right away.
  • Clarify. Ask what made them switch, and when their new agreement or contract review date is.
  • Respond by planting the seed for the future, let them know you are local and will be a resource whenever it is the right time to take a look.
  • Confirm a light follow-up, not a hard close today.
Practice It
Role-play turning "not now" into a warm, scheduled future touchpoint instead of a dead end.
