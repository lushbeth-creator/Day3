Setting the Stage
Elements of Effective Appointment Setting

The purpose of this section is to help you be prepared to share information about Propelr and how we are different from our competitors. Our goal is to create conversation when talking to a prospect, and to set an appointment.

Building the Conversation
  • Build rapport before you get down to business
  • Follow the 80/20 rule, you should be listening 80% of the time and talking only 20%
  • Ask questions with Propelr in mind, listen for openings where our strengths solve a real problem
  • Stay curious, a genuine question beats a rehearsed pitch every time
  • End with a clear next step, know exactly when and how you are following up
Questions to Ask
  • Who currently processes your payments, and how long have you been with them?
  • What do you like about your current setup, and what would you change if you could?
  • Are you in a contract right now, and do you know when it ends?
  • Has anything changed recently in your business that affects how you take payments?
  • Who else is usually part of a decision like this?
Overcoming Concerns
  • Acknowledge the concern first, do not talk over it or rush past it
  • Ask a clarifying question to understand what is really behind the hesitation
  • Respond with a point that speaks directly to what they said, not a generic feature list
  • Confirm they feel good about it before you move the conversation forward
  • Common ones to prepare for: "I am happy with my current processor", "I do not have time for this", and "I have heard sales pitches before"
After the Conversation
  • Log the details in your CRM notes right after the conversation, while it is still fresh
  • Flag any concerns or hesitations so you are ready for them at the next touchpoint
  • Use what you learned to shape your follow-up, tailor it to their situation instead of repeating the same pitch
  • Note the decision maker if one was mentioned, along with the best way to reach them
It's Not Us vs. Them, It's Them vs. Their Competition
  • A merchant's real competition is not another processor, it is the business down the street competing for the same customers
  • Faster, more reliable equipment keeps checkout lines moving and customers coming back
  • Transparent pricing frees up cash they can reinvest in their business instead of losing it to hidden fees
  • Dependable support and uptime mean they never lose a sale to a processing problem
  • Signing with Propelr is not just a vendor switch, it is one more way to help them stay ahead of their competition
