A Signal, Not a Stop Sign
Objection Handling

The goal isn't to avoid objections, it's to respond in a way that keeps the conversation moving.

Before It Happens

Get Ahead of It

A great pitch addresses the most common concerns before they're raised. Lead with local presence and transparency and you've already answered half the objections.
In the Moment

Stay Calm, Stay Curious

Defensiveness signals insecurity. Confidence looks like acknowledging their concern and redirecting, not arguing. Authenticity beats a polished deflection every time.
Handling It in the Moment
1
"I'm happy with who I have."
→ "That's great, the best time to know your options is when you're not in a rush to make a change."
2
"I don't have time right now."
→ "Completely understand, when is a better time? I'll come back." Then actually come back.
3
"What's your rate?"
→ "Rate is one piece of the puzzle. What I'd like to do is come back and do a free business analysis."
4
"We tried switching before and it was a mess."
→ "I understand your concern. Part of what sets me apart is I stay with you to minimize friction."
